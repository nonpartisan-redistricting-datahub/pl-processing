The nonpartisan Redistricting Data Hub has analyzed the 2020 Census data and identified the fastest and slowest growing Municipalities and Counties in {{State}}. Because all states must draw State Legislative and Congressional districts that contain substantial and nearly as practicable population equality respectively, these areas highlight where district lines are certain to shift during the upcoming redistricting cycle. When referencing the tables below, please note that a municipal boundaries as defined by the Census Bureau often but may not always align with a state's municipal boundaries.

The Redistricting Data Hub was able to identify these Municipalities and Counties after the U.S. Census Bureau released the 2020 Census Data in a Legacy Format on August 12th. The RDH Data Team tabulated and validated the data and will be making it publicly available at https://www.redistrictingdatahub.org by no later than August 19th - up to seven weeks before the tabulated data will be available from the U.S. Census Bureau on September 30th.

 

The Redistricting Data Hub's website currently hosts all the data necessary for drawing legally compliant State Legislative and Congressional redistricting maps. This includes Incumbent Address data, Voter File data, Population Projections for 2021 through 2030, Precinct Boundaries joined with Election Results, Boundary data, and American Community Survey data. In addition to the data, the Redistricting Data Hub operates a Help Desk, through which friendly and knowledgeable staff can answer your technical redistricting questions; simply email info@redistrictingdatahub.org.

 

About the Redistricting Data Hub

The nonpartisan Redistricting Data Hub was founded by experts with backgrounds in pioneering redistricting legal cases, the establishment of independent redistricting commissions, and related ballot initiatives in Florida, Arizona and other states. Our goal is to provide good government groups, civic organizations, journalists, educational institutions, and members of the public the data, tools, and knowledge to participate effectively in redistricting processes by learning how to define their communities, provide meaningful public input, recognize gerrymandering, and advocate for fair and legal maps. Funding for the Redistricting Data Hub comes from the Fair Representation in Redistricting Initiative, an initiative developed by a set of foundations, with nonprofit leaders, to support fair and equitable redistricting.
